History of the Boeing 737 Aircraft

On April 9th, 1967, the first Boeing 737 took its first maiden flight and was certified by the FAA for commercial flying in December 1967. The Boeing 737's body (fuselage) is manufactured in Wichita, Kansas. The fuselage is then moved by rail to Boeing's Renton factory in Washington. This narrow-body airliner has a 3x3 seating arrangement (six abreast seating). It was designed to take on short distance routes with a lower passenger demand. As of August 2024, over 16,000 Boeing 737's have been ordered, with only delivering about 11,000. Around the world, the B737 fleet has completed more than 184 million flights.

The Boeing 737 has evolved beyond just a commercial airliner, now serving as cargo, military, and corporate aircraft as well. These airliners were divided into four generations of the Boeing 737 family: The "Original", "Classic", "Next Generation", and the "Max Series".

The 1st Boeing 737 was piloted by Boeing's Chief Test Pilot Lew Wallick and Test Pilot Brien Wygle. The B737 is known as the best-selling commercial airliner in HISTORY.
