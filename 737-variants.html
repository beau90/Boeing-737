1. The Boeing 737-700 Variant:

  1. This standard version of the B737-700 launched in November of 1993. It was first delivered in 1997.
  2. The B737-700 has a seating capacity of 126 passengers (PAX).
  3. The B737-700 requires 4,800 feet of runway to successfully take-off.
  4. A typical B737-700 costs around $89 million, which makes it the least expensive Boeing aircraft.
  5. The maximum take-off weight of the B737-700 is 154,500lbs.
  6. The empty weight of a B737-700 is around 83,000lbs.

2. The Boeing 737-800 Variant:

  1. The Boeing 737-800 launched in September of 1994. It was first delivered in 1998.
  2. This stretched out version of the 700, can seat up to 189 passengers (PAX).
  3. The B737-800 requires 5,000 feet of runway to successfully take-off.
  4. The cost of B737-800 is around $106 million.
  5. The maximum take-off weight of the B737-800 is 174,100lbs.
  6. The empty weight of the B737-800 is 90,700lbs.

3. The Boeing 737-900 Variant:

  1. The Boeing 737-900 launched in 1997 and completed its first flight in August 2000.
  2. This variant of the Boeing 737 can seat up 220 passengers (PAX).
  3. The runway length for this variant is 6,800 feet for take-off.
  4. The Boeing 737-900 costs around $112 million.
  5. The maximum take-off weight of the B737-900 is 187,600lbs.
  6. The empty weight of a B737-900 is 93,680lbs.

4. The Boeing 737 MAX-8 Variant:

  1. In January of 2016, Boeing launched the MAX 8 and completed its first flight in May of 2017.
  2. The max capacity of the Boeing 737 MAX 8 is 210 passengers (PAX).
  3. The MAX series aircraft uses 20% less fuel and is 40% quieter.
  4. This new generation of the Boeing 737 aircraft costs $121.6 million.
  5. The maximum take-off weight of the MAX 8 is 181,200lbs.
  6. The MAX 8 weighs a total of 91,800lbs when empty.
5. The Boeing 737 MAX-9 Variant:
  1. At the Renton Municipal Airport in Washington, the MAX 9's first flight was in April of 2017.
  2. Just like the Boeing 737-900, the MAX 9 can carry up to 220 passengers (PAX).
  3. The Boeing MAX 9 features new fuel-efficient engines, which allows it to fly further.
  4. In this New Generation fleet of Boeing 737's, this costs a whomping $128 million.
  5. It's maximum take-off weight is 194,700lbs.
  6. The Boeing MAX 9 when empty weighs around 156,000lbs.
