The Boeing 737 Flaws & Disasters

Electrical Issues:
- In 2021, Boeing grounded 100 of its 737 MAX aircrafts due to an issue with the backup power control unit that is situated in the flight deck. This flaw could have affected the operation of many crucial systems like engine ice protection. This was due to improperly installed wiring throughout the aircraft.
Design & Manufacturing Issues:
- In 2024, The door plug of an Alaska Airlines flight ejected during flight which caused the aircraft to experience an uncontrolled decompression. This flaw was caused by 4 missing bolts. Another incident occurred with the Rudder Control System, where Boeing discovered a bolt with a improperly-tightened/missing nut.
Fuselage Issues:
- In February of 2024, Boeing found that the window frames of the Boeing 737 had incorrectly drilled holes. Although the flaw was discovered, it has said that the issue does not pose a immediate safety risk to any flying B737 aircraft. This flaw has caused over 50 aircraft deliveries to be postponed.
Southwest Airlines Flight 1248:
- In December of 2005, Southwest Airlines #1248 skidded off a runway upon landing at Chicago Midway International Airport. This incident was caused by heavy snow conditions. Minor injuries and one fatality was reported. This aircraft was a Boeing 737-700 with the registration number of N471WN. It had become N286WN after it was repaired.
Sun Country Airlines Flight 110:
- In February of 2022, Sun Country Airlines #110 suffered a right main landing gear collapse during landing at Harry Reid International Airport in Nevada. There were no reported injuries on board. The 21 year old Boeing 737-800 aircraft unfortunately sustained too much damage and has not returned to service since.
Lion Air Flight 610:
- In October of 2018, Lion Air #610 flying from Jakarta, Indonesia to Pangkal Pinang, Indonesia, crashed into the sea after only 13 minutes after takeoff. All 189 people on board the Boeing 737 MAX 8 perished. This air accident became the deadliest of all Boeing 737 variants.
